# ESG Assessment Criteria and Evidence Guide

Source basis: ESG-R certification material converted into consulting guidance for assessment, RAG retrieval, evidence mapping, gap analysis, risk assessment, and remediation planning.

Use this document to assess whether an organization has a practical, auditable ESG reporting capability rather than a narrative sustainability summary.

## Assessment Criteria

### 1. ESG governance and accountability
Requirement: The organization should define board, executive, management, and functional accountability for ESG reporting and sustainability performance.
Evidence required: Board or executive committee terms of reference, ESG committee charter, management meeting minutes, ESG roles and responsibilities, delegation matrix, internal reporting calendar.
Risk indicators: ESG is owned only by marketing or communications; no board oversight; sustainability decisions are not linked to risk, finance, strategy, operations, procurement, HR, or legal.
Audit checkpoints: Verify named owners, reporting lines, decision rights, escalation routes, committee cadence, and evidence that ESG topics are discussed by senior management.
Remediation guidance: Establish an ESG steering committee chaired by a senior executive, assign workstream owners for E, S, and G topics, and add quarterly ESG performance review to the governance calendar.

### 2. Materiality assessment
Requirement: The organization should identify material ESG topics using a documented methodology. For international readiness, cover financial materiality and impact materiality.
Evidence required: Materiality methodology, stakeholder map, workshop records, survey results, scoring matrix, materiality threshold, approved material topics list, management sign-off.
Risk indicators: ESG topics are copied from peers; no stakeholder input; no link to business model, sector risks, geography, supply chain, or investor expectations.
Audit checkpoints: Test whether selected ESG topics can be traced to evidence and whether excluded topics have documented rationale.
Remediation guidance: Run a structured materiality assessment covering stakeholder impact, financial exposure, regulatory relevance, and strategic importance.

### 3. ESG reporting framework alignment
Requirement: ESG disclosures should be mapped to the frameworks relevant to the organization, such as GRI, ISSB IFRS S1/S2, TCFD, CDP, ESRS, local exchange guidance, lender requirements, and customer requirements.
Evidence required: Framework applicability assessment, disclosure index, reporting boundary, topic mapping, crosswalk between standards, management approval.
Risk indicators: The report uses ESG language without framework mapping; climate disclosures ignore TCFD/ISSB pillars; social and governance data is incomplete.
Audit checkpoints: Confirm the report contains a disclosure basis, framework index, reporting period, boundary, definitions, and omitted-topic explanation.
Remediation guidance: Create an ESG framework matrix showing mandatory, voluntary, customer, investor, and lender disclosure requirements.

### 4. Data collection and internal controls
Requirement: ESG data should be collected through controlled processes similar to financial reporting discipline.
Evidence required: Data owners, data collection templates, calculation sheets, system extracts, review workflow, approval logs, version control, change logs, evidence repository.
Risk indicators: Spreadsheet-only process without controls; no source evidence; inconsistent units; data manually adjusted without approval; no review trail.
Audit checkpoints: Select sample KPIs and trace figures back to source records, formulas, reviewer approval, and final report.
Remediation guidance: Build an ESG data control framework with defined preparer, reviewer, approver, source system, calculation logic, and evidence retention rules.

### 5. Climate-related disclosure readiness
Requirement: The organization should disclose climate governance, strategy, risk management, metrics, targets, Scope 1 emissions, Scope 2 emissions, and material Scope 3 emissions where relevant.
Evidence required: GHG inventory, emission factor methodology, utility bills, fuel records, refrigerant logs, fleet data, supplier data requests, climate risk register, transition plan, target approval.
Risk indicators: Climate report is qualitative only; emissions data is not available; targets are unsupported; Scope 3 is ignored without materiality rationale.
Audit checkpoints: Verify emissions boundary, calculation methodology, source data completeness, emission factors, and approval of targets.
Remediation guidance: Develop a GHG accounting workbook, assign emissions data owners, collect Scope 1 and 2 source data monthly, and phase Scope 3 supplier data collection.

### 6. Social performance reporting
Requirement: Social disclosures should address workforce, health and safety, training, diversity, labor practices, human rights, supply chain responsibility, customer impacts, and community where material.
Evidence required: HR reports, training records, incident logs, grievance records, supplier labor questionnaires, human rights policy, workforce demographic data, customer complaint analysis.
Risk indicators: Social section contains values statements but no metrics; no employee data controls; supplier labor risk is not assessed.
Audit checkpoints: Trace selected social metrics to HR, HSE, procurement, legal, or customer systems and confirm consistent definitions.
Remediation guidance: Define a social KPI register and align workforce, supplier, and customer metrics to material ESG topics.

### 7. Governance and business conduct reporting
Requirement: Governance disclosures should cover ethics, anti-corruption, conflicts of interest, whistleblowing, board oversight, internal controls, risk management, and compliance.
Evidence required: Code of conduct, anti-bribery policy, training completion, whistleblowing log, investigation register, board composition, conflict declarations, internal audit reports.
Risk indicators: No anti-corruption evidence; whistleblowing channel exists but no reporting; governance claims are not supported by controls.
Audit checkpoints: Verify policy approval, training coverage, incident handling, board oversight, and corrective action closure.
Remediation guidance: Integrate ESG governance with compliance and internal audit programs.

### 8. Assurance readiness
Requirement: ESG disclosures should be prepared for limited assurance and future reasonable assurance where applicable.
Evidence required: Assurance readiness checklist, control matrix, evidence repository, data lineage, management representation process, internal audit testing, external assurance findings.
Risk indicators: No audit trail; no review controls; inconsistent figures across reports; management cannot explain calculation methodology.
Audit checkpoints: Test whether each material disclosure has source evidence, calculation logic, owner sign-off, and reviewer approval.
Remediation guidance: Perform a pre-assurance diagnostic and close control gaps before external reporting.

### 9. Digital reporting and data quality
Requirement: ESG reporting should move toward structured, repeatable, digitally traceable data rather than static manual reporting.
Evidence required: ESG data platform, controlled spreadsheets, BI dashboards, system extracts, taxonomy mapping, data dictionary, user access control.
Risk indicators: Multiple versions of ESG numbers; no data dictionary; no retention rules; no technology roadmap.
Audit checkpoints: Confirm consistent KPI names, units, formulas, reporting periods, and source systems.
Remediation guidance: Create an ESG data dictionary and prioritize automation for emissions, workforce, supplier, and governance indicators.

### 10. ESG action planning and continuous improvement
Requirement: ESG findings should lead to targets, initiatives, owners, budgets, timelines, and progress monitoring.
Evidence required: ESG roadmap, target register, action tracker, budget approvals, project charters, KPI dashboards, management status reports.
Risk indicators: Report identifies issues but no action plan; targets are not funded; no owner or target date.
Audit checkpoints: Verify that gaps, risks, initiatives, owners, and KPIs are connected.
Remediation guidance: Convert ESG gaps into a prioritized implementation roadmap with 30, 90, 180, and 365 day actions.

## Evidence Strength Rules

No Evidence: No document or data source supports the criterion.
Weak Evidence: A policy or narrative exists but no owner, process, data, or approval evidence is available.
Partial Evidence: Some documents exist, but evidence is incomplete, outdated, unaudited, or limited to one function.
Moderate Evidence: Defined process, named owners, relevant source data, and partial review trail exist.
Strong Evidence: Complete evidence pack, current controls, management approval, KPI monitoring, and corrective action process exist.
Best Practice Evidence: Evidence is digitally traceable, internally tested, externally assured or assurance-ready, and integrated into governance and enterprise risk management.

## Gap Analysis Prompts

- Which ESG topics are material and how was that decision made?
- Which ESG disclosures are mandatory, voluntary, investor-driven, customer-driven, or lender-driven?
- Which KPI values can be traced to source evidence?
- Which ESG data points have preparer, reviewer, and approver controls?
- Which statements could be challenged as greenwashing because evidence is weak?
- Which climate, social, or governance risks are missing from the risk register?
- Which remediation actions have owners, budgets, and target dates?
