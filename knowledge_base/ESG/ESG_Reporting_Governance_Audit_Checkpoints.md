# ESG Reporting Governance and Audit Checkpoints

Source basis: ESG-R certification material converted into assessment-ready consulting guidance.

This document supports RAG retrieval for ESG governance, reporting obligations, audit testing, evidence mapping, and remediation planning.

## Governance Requirements

### Board and executive oversight
Assessment expectation: ESG reporting and sustainability risks should be overseen by the board, a board committee, executive committee, or equivalent senior governance body.
Required evidence: Board agenda, sustainability committee charter, ESG dashboard, board papers, approved ESG strategy, risk appetite statement, minutes showing challenge and decisions.
Audit checkpoint: Confirm ESG is not only a communications exercise. Verify that leadership reviews material risks, climate matters, targets, assurance status, and remediation.
Risk indicator: Senior leadership approves a sustainability report without evidence of reviewing data quality, risk implications, or regulatory obligations.
Remediation action: Add ESG reporting and climate risk as standing governance agenda items with clear owner submissions.

### Cross-functional ESG operating model
Assessment expectation: ESG reporting should involve finance, legal, risk, procurement, HR, operations, HSE, facilities, IT, investor relations, and internal audit where relevant.
Required evidence: RACI matrix, workstream tracker, meeting minutes, data owner list, escalation process, reporting timetable.
Audit checkpoint: Interview key data owners and confirm they understand their ESG responsibilities and evidence requirements.
Risk indicator: ESG team collects data informally by email with no control owner accountability.
Remediation action: Establish ESG reporting workstreams with named data owners and monthly reporting deadlines.

### ESG policy architecture
Assessment expectation: Policies should cover sustainability, environment, climate, human rights, supplier conduct, ethics, anti-corruption, diversity, health and safety, whistleblowing, and data governance where material.
Required evidence: Approved policies, review dates, communication records, training logs, supplier acknowledgement, exception handling.
Audit checkpoint: Check whether policy commitments are supported by KPIs and monitoring controls.
Risk indicator: Policies are high-level statements and are not embedded into procurement, operations, HR, or risk processes.
Remediation action: Convert policy commitments into procedures, controls, owner responsibilities, and KPI monitoring.

## Reporting Obligations and Disclosure Readiness

### Disclosure basis
Assessment expectation: The organization should state the reporting framework, boundary, reporting period, entities covered, exclusions, estimation methods, and restatements.
Required evidence: Reporting basis memo, consolidation boundary, legal entity list, reporting calendar, methodology notes.
Audit checkpoint: Compare the reporting boundary with financial, operational, and legal entity records.
Risk indicator: ESG report boundary is unclear or inconsistent with the business footprint.
Remediation action: Approve a reporting basis document before collecting ESG data.

### Framework index and disclosure mapping
Assessment expectation: Disclosures should be mapped to applicable frameworks such as GRI, ISSB, TCFD, CDP, ESRS, local exchange guidance, and lender or customer requirements.
Required evidence: Disclosure index, standards crosswalk, applicability assessment, gap register.
Audit checkpoint: Confirm every mandatory or selected disclosure has a responsible owner and evidence source.
Risk indicator: Report claims alignment with frameworks but lacks an index or missing-disclosure explanation.
Remediation action: Build a disclosure requirement register with status, owner, evidence, and remediation fields.

### Materiality and stakeholder engagement
Assessment expectation: Material topics should be selected through a documented assessment process.
Required evidence: Stakeholder map, interview notes, survey data, materiality scoring, workshop outputs, approved matrix, topic boundary.
Audit checkpoint: Review whether materiality scoring reflects sector, geography, regulatory risk, stakeholder concerns, and financial impact.
Risk indicator: No evidence that stakeholders, investors, employees, suppliers, or communities were considered.
Remediation action: Run a facilitated materiality refresh and retain workshop and scoring evidence.

### ESG data validation
Assessment expectation: ESG data should be validated before reporting, with controls proportionate to risk and materiality.
Required evidence: Data templates, source documents, calculation formulas, review sign-offs, variance analysis, internal audit testing.
Audit checkpoint: Select a sample of KPIs and trace them from report number to source record.
Risk indicator: Reported figures cannot be reconciled to underlying records.
Remediation action: Add data owner certification and reviewer approval for every reported KPI.

### Assurance preparation
Assessment expectation: ESG information should be prepared for limited assurance and future reasonable assurance where applicable.
Required evidence: Control matrix, evidence repository, assurance readiness assessment, prior assurance findings, management representation letter.
Audit checkpoint: Test whether evidence is complete enough for an independent assurer to reperform or verify the disclosure.
Risk indicator: Narrative disclosures are not supported by documents, calculations, or sign-offs.
Remediation action: Create an assurance evidence pack organized by disclosure and KPI.

## Audit Checkpoint Library

### Documentation audit
Checkpoints:
- Is there an approved ESG reporting basis?
- Is there a full disclosure requirement register?
- Are material ESG topics approved by management?
- Are calculation methods documented for all quantitative KPIs?
- Are source documents retained for each reported value?
- Are changes and restatements logged?

### Governance audit
Checkpoints:
- Is ESG oversight assigned to a governance body?
- Are roles and responsibilities documented?
- Are meeting minutes retained?
- Are ESG risks escalated to ERM?
- Is internal audit involved in control testing?
- Is ESG training completed by relevant data owners?

### Data audit
Checkpoints:
- Are units of measure consistent?
- Are emission factors and conversion assumptions documented?
- Are workforce, supplier, and governance data sources defined?
- Are manual adjustments reviewed and approved?
- Are gaps and estimates disclosed?
- Is evidence traceable and version controlled?

### Greenwashing and credibility audit
Checkpoints:
- Are claims specific, measurable, and evidence-backed?
- Are targets supported by a credible plan?
- Are exclusions disclosed?
- Are offsets, renewable energy claims, or carbon reduction claims supported?
- Is marketing language consistent with operational evidence?

## Red Flag Risk Indicators

- ESG report contains broad claims but no data tables.
- Emissions, workforce, supplier, or governance data lacks source evidence.
- Materiality is not documented.
- ESG report is not reviewed by finance, legal, risk, or internal audit.
- Scope 3 is excluded without rationale.
- ESG targets exist but no roadmap, budget, owner, or interim milestone.
- Reported numbers differ across annual report, website, lender questionnaire, and customer submissions.
- Supplier ESG risk is ignored although procurement spend is material.

## Practical Remediation Plan Template

Gap: Missing ESG reporting basis.
Risk: Inconsistent and non-assurable disclosure boundary.
Root cause: No formal ESG reporting governance process.
Impact: Regulatory, investor, customer, and greenwashing exposure.
Recommendation: Approve a reporting basis memo covering framework, boundary, entities, period, methodology, exclusions, and controls.
Priority: High.
Effort: Medium.
Responsible function: ESG with Finance and Legal.
Target date: 60 days.

Gap: ESG data not assurance-ready.
Risk: Reported metrics may be challenged or restated.
Root cause: Informal data collection and no review trail.
Impact: Assurance failure, loss of stakeholder trust, compliance exposure.
Recommendation: Build KPI control sheets with source, preparer, reviewer, approver, formula, evidence link, and variance check.
Priority: High.
Effort: High.
Responsible function: ESG Reporting, Finance, Internal Audit.
Target date: 90 days.
