# ESG Risk, Maturity, and Remediation Guide

Source basis: ESG-R certification material converted into practical consulting guidance.

This guide supports assessment scoring, maturity evaluation, risk assessment, and remediation planning for ESG reporting and ESG management systems.

## ESG Risk Indicators

### Regulatory and compliance risk
Indicators:
- Mandatory ESG or climate reporting obligations are not mapped.
- Disclosures are inconsistent with local exchange, lender, investor, customer, or international framework expectations.
- ESG report does not include reporting basis, boundary, materiality process, or framework index.
- Assurance requirements are approaching but controls are not ready.
Evidence to request: Compliance obligations register, disclosure map, ESG reporting calendar, legal review notes, assurance plan.
Remediation: Build an ESG compliance obligations register and assign each requirement to an owner, evidence source, status, and due date.

### Greenwashing and credibility risk
Indicators:
- Sustainability claims are vague, broad, or not evidence-backed.
- Targets lack baseline, methodology, interim milestones, or funded initiatives.
- Carbon neutral, net zero, renewable, sustainable, ethical, or responsible claims are not supported by traceable records.
Evidence to request: Marketing claims register, target approval documents, emissions calculations, procurement records, certification evidence, assurance findings.
Remediation: Establish a claims review process involving ESG, Legal, Finance, and Communications.

### Data quality risk
Indicators:
- ESG numbers are manually compiled without controls.
- Units, boundaries, and calculation methods vary across functions.
- Data owners cannot provide source records.
- Estimates are used without disclosure or approval.
Evidence to request: Data dictionary, KPI control sheets, source evidence, approval logs, system extracts, data quality checks.
Remediation: Implement an ESG data governance model with source, formula, owner, reviewer, approver, and evidence-retention fields.

### Climate transition and physical risk
Indicators:
- Climate risks are not included in ERM.
- No Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions inventory.
- No assessment of material Scope 3 categories.
- No climate scenario analysis or transition plan.
Evidence to request: Climate risk register, emissions inventory, energy and fuel records, supplier data requests, transition roadmap, adaptation plan.
Remediation: Create a climate risk and emissions workstream aligned with TCFD and ISSB pillars.

### Supplier and value chain ESG risk
Indicators:
- Supplier ESG screening is not performed for critical or high-risk suppliers.
- Supplier code of conduct is not acknowledged.
- Human rights, labor, environmental, and anti-corruption risks are not assessed.
Evidence to request: Supplier ESG questionnaire, supplier code acceptance, audit reports, risk scoring, corrective action tracker.
Remediation: Integrate ESG screening into supplier onboarding, tender evaluation, and supplier performance management.

## ESG Maturity Model

### Level 1: Basic
Characteristics:
- ESG reporting is narrative and ad hoc.
- No formal materiality assessment.
- Data collected manually without controls.
- Governance is unclear.
Typical score: 0 to 20.
Priority actions: Define ESG owner, create disclosure register, collect baseline data, and document reporting boundary.

### Level 2: Developing
Characteristics:
- Some ESG KPIs and policies exist.
- Materiality and framework mapping are partial.
- ESG governance meetings occur but are not fully embedded.
- Data sources are inconsistent.
Typical score: 21 to 40.
Priority actions: Formalize materiality, map frameworks, assign data owners, build evidence repository.

### Level 3: Defined
Characteristics:
- ESG governance, material topics, KPIs, and reporting timetable are documented.
- Data controls exist for major KPIs.
- Report aligns to selected frameworks.
- Initial assurance readiness work has started.
Typical score: 41 to 60.
Priority actions: Strengthen control testing, improve Scope 3 and supplier ESG data, integrate ESG risks into ERM.

### Level 4: Managed
Characteristics:
- ESG reporting is governed through regular executive review.
- KPIs are monitored with clear owners and remediation actions.
- ESG data is traceable and internally tested.
- Climate, social, governance, and supplier risks are integrated into risk management.
Typical score: 61 to 80.
Priority actions: Conduct assurance readiness review, automate data collection, improve scenario analysis and supplier ESG coverage.

### Level 5: Optimized
Characteristics:
- ESG data is digitally managed, assurance-ready, and used for strategic decisions.
- ESG reporting supports investor, customer, lender, regulator, and board needs.
- Targets are science-informed or methodologically robust where applicable.
- ESG performance drives procurement, operations, finance, HR, risk, and innovation decisions.
Typical score: 81 to 100.
Priority actions: Expand external assurance, benchmark performance, integrate ESG into capital allocation and supplier collaboration.

## Remediation Planning Rules

For every ESG gap, generate:
- Risk: What could go wrong if the gap remains unresolved.
- Root cause: Governance, process, data, system, people, or evidence weakness.
- Impact: Compliance, financial, operational, reputational, investor, lender, customer, or assurance impact.
- Recommendation: Specific action to close the gap.
- Priority: Critical, High, Medium, or Low.
- Estimated effort: Low, Medium, or High.
- Responsible function: ESG, Finance, Legal, Risk, HR, Procurement, Operations, HSE, IT, Internal Audit, or Board.
- Target date: 30, 60, 90, 180, or 365 days depending on risk severity.

## Common ESG Gaps and Remediation Actions

Gap: No materiality assessment.
Risk: Reporting may omit material topics or over-report immaterial topics.
Root cause: No structured stakeholder and business impact assessment.
Impact: Weak compliance readiness, poor investor confidence, poor prioritization.
Recommendation: Conduct a materiality assessment using stakeholder input, sector risk, regulatory obligations, and financial exposure.
Priority: High.
Effort: Medium.
Responsible function: ESG with Strategy and Risk.
Target date: 90 days.

Gap: No ESG data control framework.
Risk: Data errors, restatements, assurance failure, and greenwashing exposure.
Root cause: ESG reporting process not designed like financial reporting.
Impact: Reduced credibility and potential compliance exposure.
Recommendation: Create ESG KPI control sheets, evidence folders, owner sign-offs, and internal review workflow.
Priority: High.
Effort: High.
Responsible function: ESG Reporting, Finance, Internal Audit.
Target date: 120 days.

Gap: Climate reporting is qualitative only.
Risk: Non-compliance with climate disclosure expectations and inability to satisfy investor or customer requests.
Root cause: No GHG inventory and no climate data owners.
Impact: Weak ISSB/TCFD readiness and poor transition planning.
Recommendation: Build Scope 1 and Scope 2 baseline, assess material Scope 3 categories, and create climate risk register.
Priority: High.
Effort: High.
Responsible function: ESG, Operations, Facilities, Procurement.
Target date: 180 days.

Gap: Supplier ESG risk not assessed.
Risk: Human rights, environmental, labor, ethics, and Scope 3 exposure in the value chain.
Root cause: Supplier onboarding and tender evaluation do not include ESG controls.
Impact: Customer, investor, reputation, and compliance risk.
Recommendation: Add supplier ESG questionnaire, risk scoring, code acceptance, and corrective action tracking.
Priority: Medium.
Effort: Medium.
Responsible function: Procurement and ESG.
Target date: 120 days.

Gap: ESG targets lack implementation roadmap.
Risk: Targets may be seen as unsupported claims.
Root cause: Strategy not translated into initiatives, budgets, and accountable owners.
Impact: Greenwashing risk and poor performance delivery.
Recommendation: Convert targets into funded initiatives with baseline, owner, milestone, KPI, and evidence.
Priority: High.
Effort: Medium.
Responsible function: ESG, Finance, Operations.
Target date: 90 days.
