# UAE Climate Law Governance, Reporting, and Audit Checkpoints

Source basis: UAE Climate Law Diploma material converted into consulting guidance.

This document is designed for RAG retrieval, evidence mapping, compliance assessment, audit testing, risk assessment, and remediation planning.

## Governance Requirements

### Climate officer or accountable function
Assessment expectation: The organization should assign accountable responsibility for climate compliance and reporting.
Required evidence: Appointment letter, job description, RACI matrix, reporting line, committee terms of reference, escalation process.
Audit checkpoint: Confirm the accountable person has authority to request data, escalate issues, coordinate departments, and maintain compliance evidence.
Risk indicator: Climate reporting is handled informally by ESG staff without authority over operations, finance, facilities, procurement, or risk.
Remediation action: Appoint a climate compliance officer and define authority, reporting line, and decision rights.

### Cross-functional climate committee
Assessment expectation: Climate compliance should be governed through a cross-functional committee or equivalent management forum.
Required evidence: Committee charter, membership list, meeting calendar, minutes, action tracker, KPI dashboard.
Audit checkpoint: Verify representation from ESG, legal, finance, risk, operations, facilities, HSE, procurement, HR, IT, and internal audit where relevant.
Risk indicator: Climate decisions are disconnected from capital planning, operations, supplier management, and risk management.
Remediation action: Establish a quarterly climate compliance committee with action tracking and executive escalation.

### Internal controls and data accountability
Assessment expectation: Climate data should be prepared, reviewed, approved, retained, and auditable.
Required evidence: Control matrix, data owner list, source data checklist, calculation methodology, reviewer sign-offs, change log.
Audit checkpoint: Select emissions data samples and verify preparer, reviewer, approver, source evidence, and formula.
Risk indicator: Reported emissions are compiled through uncontrolled spreadsheets and undocumented assumptions.
Remediation action: Implement climate data controls aligned with financial reporting discipline.

### Training and capacity building
Assessment expectation: Relevant personnel should understand climate law responsibilities, data requirements, evidence retention, and reporting deadlines.
Required evidence: Training plan, attendance logs, training materials, assessment results, refresher schedule.
Audit checkpoint: Interview data owners to confirm they understand what data they provide, when, and how it is evidenced.
Risk indicator: Data owners do not understand Scope 1, Scope 2, emission factors, reporting deadlines, or regulator inspection expectations.
Remediation action: Deliver role-based training for climate officer, facilities, fleet, finance, procurement, operations, HSE, and internal audit.

## Reporting Obligations

### Emissions inventory
Assessment expectation: The emissions inventory should include direct emissions, purchased energy emissions, and material value chain emissions where applicable.
Required evidence: Scope 1 records, Scope 2 electricity records, material Scope 3 assessment, emission factor source, calculations, boundary definition.
Audit checkpoint: Confirm that emissions sources are complete for owned and controlled activities.
Risk indicator: Fuel, fleet, refrigerant, process, or purchased energy data is missing.
Remediation action: Build an emissions source register and monthly data collection process.

### Reporting calendar and submission workflow
Assessment expectation: The organization should maintain a reporting calendar for climate-law submissions and internal review deadlines.
Required evidence: Compliance calendar, data collection timetable, submission workflow, approval matrix, regulator correspondence.
Audit checkpoint: Verify that all deadlines have owners and escalation triggers.
Risk indicator: Reporting is performed only at year end with no interim controls.
Remediation action: Create quarterly internal reporting and annual regulator submission readiness milestones.

### Mitigation reporting
Assessment expectation: Mitigation actions should be linked to emissions baseline, targets, initiatives, progress, and evidence.
Required evidence: Mitigation plan, reduction target, abatement initiatives, energy audits, capex approvals, performance dashboard.
Audit checkpoint: Confirm that reduction claims are quantified and supported by source evidence.
Risk indicator: Claims of emissions reduction cannot be tied to measured reductions or implementation evidence.
Remediation action: Create an abatement initiative tracker with tCO2e impact, owner, cost, status, and evidence.

### Adaptation reporting
Assessment expectation: Adaptation plans should address climate-related physical risks and operational resilience.
Required evidence: Physical climate risk assessment, adaptation plan, site action plans, BCP updates, insurance review, supplier resilience actions.
Audit checkpoint: Verify site-specific risk analysis for heat, water scarcity, flooding, extreme weather, asset vulnerability, and supply chain disruption.
Risk indicator: Adaptation plan is generic and not linked to actual assets or operations.
Remediation action: Conduct site vulnerability assessment and integrate adaptation controls into operations and business continuity.

## Mock Compliance Inspection Checklist

### Opening session
Checkpoints:
- Is there an appointed climate officer?
- Is the compliance scope clearly explained?
- Can management describe key obligations and deadlines?
- Are all requested documents available?

### Document review
Checkpoints:
- Legal obligation register exists and is current.
- GHG inventory is complete and approved.
- Scope 1 and Scope 2 source evidence is retained.
- Material Scope 3 assessment is documented.
- Mitigation and adaptation plans are approved.
- Climate risk register is active.
- Committee minutes and training records are retained.
- Prior findings and corrective actions are tracked.

### Interviews
Questions:
- Who validates emissions data before submission?
- How are emission factors selected and approved?
- What changes were made to operations because of the climate law?
- How are new MOCCAE or local authority requirements monitored?
- What is each department's role in the mitigation or adaptation plan?
- How are non-compliance risks escalated?

### Site inspection
Checkpoints:
- Fuel records and meters can be reconciled to emissions data.
- Energy efficiency measures are visible or documented.
- Refrigerant and fleet records are maintained where relevant.
- Waste, water, or process data is available where relevant.
- Physical risk controls are implemented at vulnerable locations.

### Closing and reporting
Required output:
- Findings by pillar: documentation, reporting, governance, risk, mitigation, adaptation, and inspection readiness.
- Severity: Critical, High, Medium, Low.
- Evidence observed.
- Gap and root cause.
- Recommendation.
- Responsible function.
- Target date.

## Red Flag Risk Indicators

- Missed or unclear reporting deadlines.
- Incomplete emissions inventory.
- No climate officer or committee.
- No evidence that climate risk is in ERM.
- Mitigation targets without quantified initiatives.
- Adaptation plan not linked to site risk.
- No training records.
- No internal audit or mock inspection.
- Inability to produce evidence on request.
- Repeated open corrective actions.
