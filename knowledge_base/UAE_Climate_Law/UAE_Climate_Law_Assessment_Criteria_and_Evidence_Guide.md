# UAE Climate Law Assessment Criteria and Evidence Guide

Source basis: UAE Climate Law Diploma material converted into assessment-ready consulting guidance for Federal Decree-Law No. 11 of 2024 climate compliance.

Use this guide to assess an organization's readiness for UAE climate-law compliance, emissions reporting, mitigation planning, adaptation planning, governance, risk management, inspection readiness, and remediation.

## Assessment Criteria

### 1. Climate compliance applicability and obligation mapping
Requirement: The organization should determine whether and how UAE climate-law requirements apply to its entities, facilities, activities, emissions profile, sector, and emirate-level obligations.
Evidence required: Legal applicability assessment, entity and facility register, sector classification, regulatory obligations register, local emirate requirements, compliance calendar.
Risk indicators: No legal applicability review; no owner for monitoring MOCCAE and local authority requirements; operations across emirates are treated as one generic compliance profile.
Audit checkpoints: Verify that all UAE entities and facilities are included and that obligations are mapped by article, regulator, frequency, owner, evidence, and deadline.
Remediation guidance: Create a UAE climate compliance obligations register with article-level requirements, owners, evidence, and due dates.

### 2. GHG emissions inventory and reporting
Requirement: The organization should maintain a greenhouse gas inventory covering Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions and material Scope 3 emissions where applicable.
Evidence required: GHG inventory, organizational boundary, operational boundary, fuel records, utility bills, refrigerant logs, fleet data, process emissions data, emission factors, calculation methodology, reviewer sign-off.
Risk indicators: Emissions data is estimated without methodology; energy bills are not retained; Scope 1 and Scope 2 are incomplete; Scope 3 materiality is not assessed.
Audit checkpoints: Trace reported emissions to source data and confirm calculation method, emission factors, period, boundary, and approval trail.
Remediation guidance: Build a controlled emissions inventory workbook and assign facility, fleet, finance, procurement, and ESG data owners.

### 3. Climate reporting submission readiness
Requirement: Climate reports, GHG inventory data, and related compliance submissions should be prepared for the National Climate Registry or relevant regulator submission process.
Evidence required: Reporting calendar, submission account or portal responsibility, draft submissions, management approval, evidence pack, confirmation receipts, regulatory correspondence.
Risk indicators: No submission workflow; no record of who approves data; submission deadlines are not monitored.
Audit checkpoints: Verify that reports can be reproduced from retained source data and that submission responsibility is assigned.
Remediation guidance: Create a climate reporting standard operating procedure covering preparation, review, approval, submission, and records retention.

### 4. Emissions mitigation strategy
Requirement: The organization should identify major emissions sources and maintain a mitigation plan aligned with national decarbonization priorities and sector pathways.
Evidence required: Emissions baseline, emissions hotspot analysis, reduction initiatives, abatement plan, target register, initiative business cases, energy audits, progress dashboard.
Risk indicators: Reduction targets are stated but not supported by quantified initiatives; no baseline year; no link between emissions sources and actions.
Audit checkpoints: Verify that initiatives have owner, cost, expected tCO2e impact, timeline, and progress evidence.
Remediation guidance: Develop a mitigation roadmap covering energy efficiency, renewable energy, fleet transition, process change, procurement, supplier engagement, and behavior change.

### 5. Climate adaptation strategy
Requirement: The organization should assess physical climate risks and maintain adaptation actions for assets, operations, supply chain, workforce, and business continuity.
Evidence required: Physical climate risk assessment, site vulnerability analysis, heat risk assessment, water stress assessment, flood or extreme weather analysis, business continuity updates, adaptation plan.
Risk indicators: No assessment of heat, water scarcity, flooding, supply chain disruption, or asset vulnerability; adaptation plan is generic.
Audit checkpoints: Confirm risks are assessed by location, operation, impact, likelihood, control, owner, and review date.
Remediation guidance: Conduct a physical climate risk assessment and integrate adaptation actions into facilities, HSE, operations, procurement, and business continuity plans.

### 6. Climate governance under Article 14
Requirement: The organization should establish internal governance for climate compliance, including accountable officers, committees, training, internal controls, and reporting escalation.
Evidence required: Climate officer appointment, ESG or climate committee charter, meeting minutes, RACI matrix, policy, training records, internal control matrix, reporting dashboard.
Risk indicators: One person informally handles climate compliance without authority; climate governance is not linked to finance, operations, procurement, legal, risk, or internal audit.
Audit checkpoints: Interview accountable officers and functional owners to test awareness, responsibilities, and evidence control.
Remediation guidance: Appoint a climate compliance officer, establish a cross-functional committee, and integrate climate compliance into enterprise governance.

### 7. Climate risk integration with ERM
Requirement: Climate-related physical and transition risks should be integrated into the enterprise risk management process.
Evidence required: Climate risk register, ERM risk entries, risk scoring method, risk owners, mitigation controls, monitoring dashboard, board or executive risk reporting.
Risk indicators: Climate risk is treated as a sustainability narrative rather than an enterprise risk; no owner or scoring; no risk response.
Audit checkpoints: Confirm climate risks have likelihood, impact, controls, owner, target date, and escalation threshold.
Remediation guidance: Add climate risks to ERM and align them with strategy, operations, finance, procurement, legal, insurance, and business continuity.

### 8. Legal risk register and enforcement preparedness
Requirement: The organization should maintain a climate-law legal risk register covering missed reporting, inaccurate data, weak governance, incomplete mitigation, incomplete adaptation, and inspection readiness.
Evidence required: Legal risk register, compliance gap assessment, risk scoring, action tracker, regulator correspondence log, internal audit reports.
Risk indicators: No article-level gap assessment; no documented response to non-compliance risks; prior findings remain open.
Audit checkpoints: Verify risks are updated periodically and reviewed by ESG committee, legal, risk, or executive management.
Remediation guidance: Build a legal risk register with risk ID, article reference, description, owner, likelihood, impact, controls, response, status, and evidence.

### 9. Audit and inspection readiness
Requirement: The organization should be ready for regulator information requests, mock inspections, internal audit reviews, and third-party verification where required.
Evidence required: Mock audit report, document request list, evidence repository, interview scripts, site inspection records, corrective action tracker, management response.
Risk indicators: Evidence is scattered; departments cannot explain their role; emissions numbers cannot be supported; corrective actions are not tracked.
Audit checkpoints: Conduct walkthroughs with ESG, legal, finance, operations, facilities, procurement, and internal audit.
Remediation guidance: Run a mock climate compliance inspection and close documentation, governance, and risk findings.

### 10. Strategic value and early compliance
Requirement: The organization should use compliance readiness to support investor confidence, trade access, supply chain qualification, customer requirements, financing, and reputation.
Evidence required: Climate compliance roadmap, green finance readiness, supplier/customer ESG responses, transition plan, performance dashboard, public claims review.
Risk indicators: Compliance is treated only as a legal burden; no link to tenders, financing, customer requirements, or brand claims.
Audit checkpoints: Check whether compliance evidence supports market-facing claims and customer or lender due diligence.
Remediation guidance: Convert climate compliance into a strategic roadmap with regulatory, operational, financial, and commercial benefits.

## Evidence Strength Rules

No Evidence: No climate-law document, data, owner, or process exists.
Weak Evidence: Narrative policy exists but no article mapping, source data, owner, or approval evidence.
Partial Evidence: Some reporting or emissions documents exist but are incomplete, inconsistent, or not governance-approved.
Moderate Evidence: Defined process, current source data, named owners, and partial review trail exist.
Strong Evidence: Complete evidence pack, approved governance, controlled calculations, risk register, action plan, and reporting calendar exist.
Best Practice Evidence: Digitally traceable data, internal audit testing, mock inspection, external assurance readiness, ERM integration, and board oversight exist.

## Gap Analysis Prompts

- Has the organization mapped UAE climate-law obligations by article, entity, facility, owner, evidence, and deadline?
- Are Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions measured using reliable source data and documented emission factors?
- Has material Scope 3 been assessed?
- Is there a mitigation plan linked to the emissions baseline and national decarbonization direction?
- Is there an adaptation plan linked to physical climate risk?
- Has a climate officer or committee been formally appointed?
- Are climate risks included in ERM?
- Is the organization ready for regulator inspection or information requests?
