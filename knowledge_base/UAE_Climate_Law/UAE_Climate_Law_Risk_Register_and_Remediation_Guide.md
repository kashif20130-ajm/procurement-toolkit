# UAE Climate Law Risk Register and Remediation Guide

Source basis: UAE Climate Law Diploma material converted into practical consulting guidance.

Use this document to generate climate-law risk registers, gap remediation plans, audit findings, risk ratings, and action plans.

## Climate-Law Risk Register Structure

Each risk entry should include:
- Risk ID.
- Article or obligation reference.
- Risk description.
- Root cause.
- Likelihood.
- Impact.
- Inherent risk rating.
- Existing controls.
- Control effectiveness.
- Residual risk rating.
- Risk owner.
- Remediation action.
- Target date.
- Evidence required.
- Status.

## Core Risk Categories

### 1. Emissions reporting risk
Risk: GHG inventory is incomplete, inaccurate, unsupported, or submitted late.
Root causes: Missing source data, weak boundary definition, no emission factor methodology, no owner, no review control.
Impact: Regulatory non-compliance, penalties, restatement, reputational damage, loss of stakeholder confidence.
Evidence required: Emissions inventory, fuel and utility records, calculation workbook, emission factor source, reviewer approval, submission confirmation.
Recommended controls: Monthly data collection, quarterly review, annual internal audit, evidence repository, emission source register.

### 2. Mitigation non-compliance risk
Risk: The organization cannot demonstrate credible emissions reduction planning or progress.
Root causes: No baseline, no quantified abatement initiatives, no budget, weak target governance.
Impact: Compliance weakness, greenwashing exposure, missed decarbonization opportunities, investor and customer risk.
Evidence required: Baseline, mitigation roadmap, target register, initiative business cases, energy audit, progress dashboard.
Recommended controls: Abatement tracker with owner, cost, tCO2e impact, status, and evidence.

### 3. Adaptation and resilience risk
Risk: Physical climate risks are not assessed or controlled.
Root causes: No site-level risk assessment, no adaptation owner, no integration with business continuity.
Impact: Asset damage, employee safety exposure, supply disruption, insurance cost increase, service interruption.
Evidence required: Physical risk assessment, site vulnerability analysis, adaptation plan, BCP updates, insurance review.
Recommended controls: Annual site risk review and integration into operations, facilities, HSE, procurement, and BCP.

### 4. Governance and accountability risk
Risk: Climate compliance has no clear owner, committee, escalation route, or internal controls.
Root causes: Climate law treated as ESG communications rather than enterprise compliance.
Impact: Missed deadlines, inconsistent data, unresolved gaps, weak regulator response.
Evidence required: Climate officer appointment, committee charter, minutes, RACI, training records, control matrix.
Recommended controls: Article 14 governance framework with cross-functional committee and executive escalation.

### 5. ERM integration risk
Risk: Climate risks are not included in the enterprise risk register.
Root causes: Risk team not involved; climate risk not translated into likelihood, impact, owner, and controls.
Impact: Understated enterprise risk, weak board reporting, poor capital allocation, poor resilience.
Evidence required: ERM entries, climate risk scoring, risk owner assignment, board risk reports.
Recommended controls: Add physical and transition risks to ERM with quarterly monitoring.

### 6. Enforcement and inspection risk
Risk: Organization cannot respond effectively to regulator inspection or information requests.
Root causes: Evidence scattered across departments, no inspection protocol, no mock audit.
Impact: Enforcement escalation, penalties, license or permit implications, reputational harm.
Evidence required: Evidence repository, document request checklist, mock audit report, corrective action tracker.
Recommended controls: Annual mock climate compliance inspection and quarterly evidence pack review.

### 7. Greenwashing and public claims risk
Risk: Climate claims are not supported by evidence or are overstated.
Root causes: Marketing, ESG, and legal reviews are not integrated; reduction claims not linked to measured data.
Impact: Reputational damage, regulator scrutiny, customer loss, investor concern.
Evidence required: Claims register, legal review, emissions evidence, target methodology, initiative progress.
Recommended controls: Climate claims approval workflow involving ESG, Legal, Finance, and Communications.

## Remediation Logic

Priority rules:
- Critical: Missed legal deadline, no emissions inventory, false or unsupported regulatory submission, regulator notice, or severe inspection failure.
- High: No governance owner, incomplete Scope 1 or Scope 2 data, no mitigation plan, no adaptation risk assessment, no ERM integration.
- Medium: Partial evidence, manual controls, delayed action closure, incomplete training, incomplete supplier or Scope 3 assessment.
- Low: Improvement opportunity with existing controls operating.

Effort rules:
- Low effort: Policy update, RACI clarification, meeting cadence, evidence folder, minor template improvement.
- Medium effort: Obligation register, data owner model, training rollout, risk register, dashboard, mock audit.
- High effort: GHG baseline, digital data system, full Scope 3 assessment, site adaptation plan, assurance readiness program.

Target date rules:
- Critical: 30 days.
- High: 60 to 90 days.
- Medium: 90 to 180 days.
- Low: 180 to 365 days.

## Common Gaps and Remediation Actions

Gap: No UAE climate-law obligations register.
Risk: Compliance deadlines and article-level requirements may be missed.
Root cause: No structured legal mapping.
Impact: Regulatory non-compliance and weak inspection readiness.
Recommendation: Build an obligation register covering article, requirement, applicable entity, owner, evidence, frequency, deadline, and status.
Priority: High.
Estimated effort: Medium.
Responsible function: Legal and ESG.
Target date: 60 days.

Gap: Scope 1 and Scope 2 inventory incomplete.
Risk: Emissions reporting is inaccurate or unsupported.
Root cause: Missing source data and no data collection control.
Impact: Submission risk, regulator challenge, restatement, credibility loss.
Recommendation: Create emissions source register and collect fuel, fleet, refrigerant, process, and electricity records monthly.
Priority: Critical.
Estimated effort: High.
Responsible function: ESG, Facilities, Operations, Finance.
Target date: 90 days.

Gap: No mitigation roadmap.
Risk: The organization cannot demonstrate credible reduction planning.
Root cause: Baseline not converted into reduction initiatives.
Impact: Compliance weakness, greenwashing risk, missed efficiency opportunities.
Recommendation: Develop a mitigation roadmap with targets, initiatives, tCO2e impact, cost, owner, milestone, and evidence.
Priority: High.
Estimated effort: High.
Responsible function: ESG, Operations, Finance.
Target date: 120 days.

Gap: No adaptation plan.
Risk: Physical climate risks remain unmanaged.
Root cause: No site-specific climate vulnerability assessment.
Impact: Asset, workforce, insurance, supply chain, and business continuity exposure.
Recommendation: Conduct physical risk assessment and integrate adaptation actions into BCP, HSE, facilities, procurement, and operations.
Priority: High.
Estimated effort: High.
Responsible function: Risk, Operations, Facilities, HSE.
Target date: 180 days.

Gap: No climate officer or cross-functional committee.
Risk: Compliance accountabilities are unclear.
Root cause: Climate law treated as a reporting task rather than governance obligation.
Impact: Missed deadlines, inconsistent data, weak escalation, poor audit response.
Recommendation: Appoint climate officer and establish cross-functional committee with monthly implementation tracking.
Priority: High.
Estimated effort: Medium.
Responsible function: Executive Management, ESG, Legal.
Target date: 60 days.

Gap: Climate risks not in ERM.
Risk: Physical and transition risks are not visible to executive management.
Root cause: No risk taxonomy or scoring method for climate risks.
Impact: Poor resilience, weak board reporting, underprepared operations.
Recommendation: Add climate risks to ERM with likelihood, impact, owner, controls, residual rating, and action plan.
Priority: High.
Estimated effort: Medium.
Responsible function: Risk Management and ESG.
Target date: 90 days.

Gap: No mock compliance inspection.
Risk: Evidence gaps may only be discovered during regulator inspection.
Root cause: No testing process.
Impact: Enforcement escalation and reputational risk.
Recommendation: Run a mock inspection covering documentation, emissions data, governance, risk, mitigation, adaptation, and interviews.
Priority: Medium.
Estimated effort: Medium.
Responsible function: Internal Audit, ESG, Legal.
Target date: 120 days.

## Maturity Model Concepts

Level 1 Basic: Compliance is informal; no complete inventory, no article mapping, no governance structure.
Level 2 Developing: Initial emissions data and governance owner exist, but evidence and controls are incomplete.
Level 3 Defined: Obligations, inventory, mitigation, adaptation, governance, and risk register are documented.
Level 4 Managed: Climate compliance is reviewed by committee, integrated into ERM, supported by internal controls, and tested by mock audit.
Level 5 Optimized: Data is digitally traceable, assurance-ready, integrated with strategy and capital planning, and used for competitive advantage.
