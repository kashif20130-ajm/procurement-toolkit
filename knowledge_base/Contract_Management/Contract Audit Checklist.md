# Contract Audit Checklist

## Purpose
Define a repeatable audit checklist for contract files, approvals, obligations, changes, and retention. The checklist helps verify that contract records are complete, searchable, approved, and ready for internal or external review.

## Scope
This checklist applies to active contracts, expired contracts within retention period, high-risk contracts, material amendments, addenda, variations, subcontracting approvals, and contracts selected for periodic file-quality review.

## Key Principles
- Audit evidence must be linked to the contract record, not stored only in email.
- Audit scope should include approvals, signed copies, obligations, renewals, and changes.
- High-risk contracts should receive deeper review than low-risk template contracts.
- Audit findings must have owners and target resolution dates.
- Retention must be checked against the 7-year minimum UAE FTA and commercial-law requirement.

## Procedure Or Framework
| Audit area | Evidence to check | Pass criteria |
|---|---|---|
| Register completeness | Contract ID, counterparty, owner, value, term, renewal, status, risk level. | Mandatory fields are complete and current. |
| Executed copy | Signed PDF or electronic signature envelope. | Final signed version is stored and linked. |
| Approval trail | DoA, finance, privacy/security, procurement, legal, exception approvals. | Approvals match value, risk, and deviation profile. |
| Change control | Addenda, variations, PO amendments, delegation or subcontracting approvals. | Changes are documented, approved, and linked to the original contract. |
| Obligations | Clause references, owner, due date, frequency, status, evidence. | Operational obligations are assigned and monitored. |
| Renewal management | End date, notice date, auto-renewal status, owner decision. | Renewal decision is made before notice deadline. |
| Retention | Archive path and retention date. | Records are retained for 7 years minimum. |

## Roles And Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Contract manager | Runs audit, samples files, records findings, tracks remediation. |
| Business owner | Confirms operational status, obligation evidence, and renewal decisions. |
| Legal counsel | Reviews legal exceptions, addenda, disputes, and high-risk deviations. |
| Finance / procurement | Confirms value, PO, payment, and DoA evidence. |
| Privacy / security | Confirms data-processing approvals and security evidence. |

## Evidence Required
- Audit sample list.
- Contract register export.
- Executed contract file.
- Approval trail.
- Obligation tracker.
- Change/addendum record.
- Renewal decision evidence.
- Audit findings log.

## Assessment Checklist
- Is the signed version linked and readable?
- Are mandatory register fields complete?
- Does approval evidence match risk and value?
- Are addenda and variations traceable?
- Are obligations assigned to real owners?
- Are overdue obligations escalated?
- Are renewal notice dates visible?
- Is retention documented?

## Common Gaps
- Missing signed copy or wrong version linked.
- Approval trail exists in email but not in the contract file.
- Contract value differs between register, PO, and executed agreement.
- Addendum signed but register not updated.
- Obligations not extracted after signature.
- Retention date missing.

## Recommended Improvements
- Audit a monthly sample of high-risk and renewal-near contracts.
- Add mandatory remediation owners and due dates.
- Use exception reporting for missing signed copies and approvals.
- Link addenda and PO changes to the original contract ID.
- Track recurring audit failures as process KPIs.
