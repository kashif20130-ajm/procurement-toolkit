# Contract Risk Matrix

## Purpose
Provide a structured method to rate contract risk and route contracts to the correct review and approval path. The matrix prevents low-value but high-risk contracts from bypassing legal, privacy, security, or executive review.

## Scope
The matrix applies to all new contracts, renewals, amendments, addenda, variations, subcontracting requests, assignments, and material PO changes. It should be used at intake and refreshed whenever contract scope, value, data use, liability, or operational criticality changes.

## Key Principles
- Rate the contract at the highest individual risk factor.
- A single high-risk factor is enough to route a contract as high risk.
- Risk is not only financial; data, liability, criticality, and template position matter.
- Risk rating must be visible in the contract register.
- Risk rating should be reassessed before signature and after material change requests.

## Procedure Or Framework
| Risk factor | Low | Medium | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contract value | Low value or no monetary commitment. | Moderate value within normal budget authority. | High value, strategic spend, or revenue impact. |
| Data and systems | No personal, confidential, or system access data. | Limited personal or confidential data. | Sensitive, regulated, cross-border, or privileged system access. |
| Liability exposure | Standard capped liability and balanced remedies. | Negotiated cap or limited carveouts. | Uncapped liability, broad indemnities, penalties, or liquidated damages. |
| Operational criticality | Non-critical, easy replacement, low dependency. | Material support or delivery dependency. | Mission-critical vendor, customer commitment, SLA, or business continuity impact. |
| Template position | Company template with minimal changes. | Counterparty paper or moderate deviations. | Heavily negotiated terms, unusual jurisdiction, or high-risk commercial model. |

Suggested rating logic:
1. Score each factor as 1 for low, 2 for medium, and 3 for high.
2. If any factor is 3, classify as high risk.
3. If no factor is 3 but the total score is 8 or more, classify as medium risk.
4. Otherwise classify as low risk.

## Roles And Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Business owner | Provides accurate value, scope, operational dependency, and timing information. |
| Contract manager | Applies the matrix and records the risk level in the register. |
| Legal counsel | Confirms risk level for non-standard terms, liability, jurisdiction, and high-risk clauses. |
| Privacy / security | Rates data, system access, subprocessors, breach, and audit risk. |
| Finance / procurement | Rates value, budget, payment, PO, and supplier concentration risk. |

## Evidence Required
- Completed intake form.
- Risk factor scores and suggested rating.
- Review lane decision.
- Legal/privacy/security notes for medium and high-risk contracts.
- Approval trail for high-risk exceptions.
- Updated register risk field.

## Assessment Checklist
- Has every risk factor been scored?
- Does the highest risk factor drive the route?
- Is the risk rating consistent with data and system access?
- Is the liability position standard or escalated?
- Does operational criticality reflect business continuity impact?
- Has the rating been refreshed after any material variation?

## Common Gaps
- Rating contracts based only on value.
- Treating DPAs or data-bearing contracts as low risk because value is zero.
- Failing to reassess risk after scope changes.
- No evidence explaining why a high-risk term was accepted.
- Missing privacy/security review for vendors with system access.

## Recommended Improvements
- Embed the matrix in the intake workflow.
- Add mandatory explanations for any high-risk score.
- Review high-risk contracts quarterly.
- Trend recurring risk drivers and update the clause playbook.
- Require executive approval for critical risk exceptions.
