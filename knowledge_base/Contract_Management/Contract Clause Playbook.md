# Contract Clause Playbook

## Purpose
Provide standard negotiation positions, fallbacks, and escalation triggers for recurring contract clauses. This playbook supports faster review while preserving consistency and controlled escalation.

## Scope
The playbook applies to legal and commercial negotiation of liability, indemnity, payment, termination, auto-renewal, intellectual property, subcontracting, assignment, data protection, and governing law clauses.

## Key Principles
- Preferred positions should be used first.
- Fallbacks require documented business and legal rationale.
- Escalation triggers must be clear enough for non-lawyers to recognize.
- Repeated exceptions should feed template updates.
- Subcontracting and assignment must be reviewed against notice, consent, and applicable Dubai Law No. 5 of 2026 requirements.

## Procedure Or Framework
| Clause | Preferred position | Fallback | Escalate when |
|---|---|---|---|
| Liability cap | Fees paid/payable in prior 12 months. | Up to 2x fees for defined claims. | Uncapped liability or broad carveouts. |
| Indemnity | Third-party IP, confidentiality, data breach caused by indemnifying party. | Mutual indemnity with procedural controls. | First-party losses or open-ended business claims. |
| Payment | Net 30 from valid invoice. | Net 45 with finance approval. | Net 60+, broad setoff, or subjective acceptance. |
| Termination | Mutual material breach after cure. | Convenience if economics are protected. | No termination right or long cure for critical breach. |
| Auto-renewal | Notice-based renewal with tracked opt-out date. | Renewal allowed with at least 60 days notice. | Notice window under 30 days or price increase without notice. |
| IP ownership | Each party keeps pre-existing IP; licenses match deal model. | Limited assignment for paid deliverables. | Broad assignment of tools, know-how, or platform IP. |
| Subcontracting / assignment | Subcontracting requires 30 days prior written notice. | Prior written notice for critical trades only. | No subcontracting allowed without consent or contract falls under Dubai Law No. 5 of 2026 requiring explicit upstream approvals via Etimad. |
| Data protection | DPA matches data map and operating capability. | Alternative clauses reviewed by privacy/security. | Unsupported transfer terms or breach notice shorter than feasible. |

## Roles And Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Legal counsel | Owns preferred positions, fallback language, and escalation decisions. |
| Business owner | Confirms commercial tolerance and operational impact. |
| Privacy / security | Reviews data protection, subprocessors, breach, and system-access terms. |
| Finance / procurement | Reviews payment, PO, credit, setoff, and commercial concessions. |
| Contract manager | Logs deviations and updates the playbook review queue. |

## Evidence Required
- Redline showing clause change.
- Playbook deviation note.
- Fallback approval.
- Escalation decision record.
- Privacy/security approval where applicable.
- Finance approval for payment deviations.
- Etimad/client approval evidence where applicable.

## Assessment Checklist
- Is the proposed clause aligned to preferred position?
- If not, is the fallback documented?
- Does the issue meet an escalation trigger?
- Has the correct approver accepted the deviation?
- Does the clause create a new obligation?
- Should the template or playbook be updated?

## Common Gaps
- Accepting counterparty fallback without documenting why.
- Escalating too late after commercial positions are already promised.
- Treating subcontracting as an operational issue only.
- Missing privacy/security review for DPA deviations.
- Not updating templates after repeated clause concessions.

## Recommended Improvements
- Review clause exceptions quarterly.
- Add approved fallback language to templates.
- Track negotiation drivers by clause type.
- Train business owners on escalation triggers.
- Require written approval for high-risk deviations.
