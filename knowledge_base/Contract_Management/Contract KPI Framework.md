# Contract KPI Framework

## Purpose
Define practical KPIs for measuring contract workflow health, audit readiness, renewal control, obligation management, and negotiation quality. The KPI framework helps leadership identify bottlenecks and recurring risk drivers.

## Scope
The framework applies to contract intake, review, approval, signature, register quality, obligation tracking, renewals, audits, exceptions, and change management. KPIs should be reviewed monthly and trended quarterly.

## Key Principles
- KPIs must drive action, not just reporting.
- Cycle-time metrics should be segmented by risk level and review lane.
- Readiness metrics should focus on evidence completeness.
- Renewal and obligation metrics should identify preventable operational risk.
- Exception trends should feed template and playbook improvements.

## Procedure Or Framework
| KPI | Definition | Target / signal |
|---|---|---|
| Cycle time | Days from intake submission to signature. | Track median and high-risk outliers monthly. |
| First-pass completeness | Percentage of intake requests accepted without missing mandatory fields. | Target 90% or higher after process stabilization. |
| Renewal notice compliance | Percentage of renewal decisions made before notice deadline. | Target 100% for auto-renewal contracts. |
| Obligation overdue rate | Open obligations past due divided by total open obligations. | Escalate when above 5%. |
| High-risk exception rate | High-risk contracts with approved deviations divided by total high-risk contracts. | Review trend and recurring clause drivers quarterly. |
| Audit readiness | Contracts with complete executed copy, approvals, owner, value, term, and retention data. | Target 95% completeness. |
| Change-control compliance | Material variations with DoA approval and written addendum. | Target 100% for variations above 10%. |

## Roles And Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Contract manager | Produces KPI dashboard, validates source data, tracks corrective actions. |
| Business owner | Resolves intake gaps, obligation delays, and renewal decisions. |
| Legal counsel | Analyzes negotiation exceptions and updates templates/playbook. |
| Finance / procurement | Tracks DoA, PO alignment, and payment-term exceptions. |
| Governance chair | Reviews KPI trends and assigns improvement actions. |

## Evidence Required
- KPI dashboard.
- Intake queue data.
- Contract register data.
- Obligation tracker data.
- Renewal calendar.
- Audit checklist results.
- High-risk exception log.
- Change-management register.

## Assessment Checklist
- Are KPIs refreshed monthly?
- Are metrics segmented by risk level?
- Are source data fields complete enough to trust the KPI?
- Are overdue obligations and renewal decisions escalated?
- Are high-risk exceptions reviewed for template improvements?
- Are KPI actions owned and tracked?

## Common Gaps
- Measuring volume but not cycle time or risk.
- KPI data polluted by incomplete register fields.
- Renewal compliance measured after deadlines pass.
- No owner assigned for improvement actions.
- High-risk exceptions approved repeatedly without playbook updates.

## Recommended Improvements
- Create a monthly dashboard with threshold indicators.
- Add data-quality checks before KPI publication.
- Trend KPIs for at least three months before setting aggressive targets.
- Link KPI exceptions to governance actions.
- Use recurring negotiation issues to revise templates and fallback language.
