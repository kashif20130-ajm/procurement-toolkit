# Contract Governance Framework

## Purpose
Define decision rights, accountability, cadence, and escalation paths for contract management. The framework clarifies who owns commercial decisions, legal positions, approvals, data risk, change control, and post-signature performance.

## Scope
The framework applies to all contract lifecycle stages: intake, risk triage, review, negotiation, approval, signature, storage, obligation tracking, renewals, variations, audits, and KPI reporting.

## Key Principles
- Governance must assign accountability, not merely participation.
- Business owners own commercial outcomes and operational delivery.
- Legal owns legal positions and deviation control.
- Finance/procurement owns budget, payment, PO, and DoA alignment.
- Contract management owns process integrity, records, renewals, and evidence.
- Covered Dubai Law No. 5 of 2026 variations and delegations require Etimad/client entity approval checks.

## Procedure Or Framework
| Governance layer | Decision rights | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Business owner | Commercial need, scope, operational acceptance, budget ownership. | Per request and monthly obligation follow-up. |
| Contract manager | Register quality, renewal alerts, workflow routing, records, audit coordination. | Weekly queue review and monthly governance pack. |
| Legal | Template control, negotiation positions, deviations, high-risk terms, addenda. | As needed for reviews; quarterly playbook refresh. |
| Finance / procurement | Value, budget, payment terms, PO alignment, DoA compliance. | Per approval threshold and monthly exceptions review. |
| Privacy / security | Data processing, system access, security controls, breach and audit terms. | Per data-bearing contract and quarterly high-risk review. |
| Executive sponsor | Strategic contracts, high-risk exceptions, disputes, material variations. | Exception-based and quarterly governance review. |
| Client entity approver | Etimad approvals for covered contracts. | Per covered variation or delegation. |

## Roles And Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Governance chair | Reviews KPIs, exceptions, overdue actions, and systemic issues. |
| Contract manager | Prepares governance pack and tracks action closure. |
| Business owner | Reports operational issues, renewals, acceptance, and supplier/customer performance. |
| Legal counsel | Reports high-risk clauses, disputes, and template improvements. |
| Finance / procurement | Reports value changes, PO exceptions, and DoA compliance. |
| Privacy / security | Reports data-bearing contract issues and control gaps. |

## Evidence Required
- Governance meeting agenda and minutes.
- Monthly contract dashboard.
- Renewal action list.
- Open obligations and overdue list.
- High-risk contract exceptions.
- Audit findings and remediation log.
- Change-control register.
- KPI trend report.

## Assessment Checklist
- Are decision rights clear by role?
- Are monthly governance packs produced?
- Are renewal and obligation exceptions escalated?
- Are high-risk deviations reviewed?
- Are action owners and due dates recorded?
- Are Dubai Law No. 5 of 2026 Etimad approval checks captured where applicable?

## Common Gaps
- Many reviewers, no accountable owner.
- Governance meeting reviews volume but not risk.
- Legal exceptions accepted without template feedback.
- Finance approval not linked to addendum or PO change.
- Etimad/client entity approval evidence not retained.
- KPI trends discussed without corrective actions.

## Recommended Improvements
- Establish a monthly governance pack with standard sections.
- Track action closure between meetings.
- Add a high-risk exception register.
- Review renewal decisions 120 days ahead.
- Require quarterly clause playbook updates from negotiation data.
- Include audit and change-control findings in governance reporting.
