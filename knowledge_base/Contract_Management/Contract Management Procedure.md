# Contract Management Procedure

## Purpose
Define a consistent end-to-end procedure for requesting, reviewing, approving, signing, storing, and monitoring contracts. The procedure is intended to reduce missed approvals, untracked obligations, unmanaged renewals, and informal changes that bypass the delegation of authority.

## Scope
This procedure applies to NDAs, MSAs, SOWs, order forms, amendments, DPAs, vendor agreements, partnership agreements, addenda, and material contract variations. It covers contracts from intake through post-signature governance, including records retention and change control.

## Key Principles
- Every contract must have a named business owner.
- A contract cannot be marked active until the executed copy, value, risk level, term, renewal date, and owner are recorded.
- Risk routing must be based on the highest material risk factor, not only contract value.
- Non-standard terms must be tracked before signature, not rediscovered during disputes.
- Contract records must be retained for 7 years minimum to fulfill UAE Federal Tax Authority (FTA) and commercial law requirements.
- Any cumulative contract variation exceeding 10% of the original contract value must trigger formal DoA re-approval and a written Addendum, rather than a simple PO amendment.

## Procedure Or Framework
1. Open a request in the intake queue with counterparty, contract type, business owner, target signature date, estimated value, data profile, and known non-standard terms.
2. Triage the request using the contract risk matrix and assign a review lane: business review, legal review, privacy/security review, finance review, or executive approval.
3. Draft or review using the approved template where possible. If counterparty paper is used, identify deviations from preferred positions.
4. Negotiate against the clause playbook. Track preferred position, fallback, escalation trigger, decision owner, and open issue status.
5. Confirm approvals before signature: legal, finance, procurement, privacy/security, executive sponsor, and DoA approver as applicable.
6. Confirm the final version is clean, complete, and includes all exhibits, schedules, addenda, and correct legal entity details.
7. Execute the contract using an authorized signatory.
8. Store the signed copy, update the contract register, extract obligations, and schedule renewal notice dates.
9. Monitor obligations, renewals, changes, audit findings, and KPI performance monthly.

## Roles And Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Business owner | Defines business need, confirms scope, accepts deliverables, owns operational performance. |
| Contract manager | Runs intake, register quality, routing, renewals, audit queue, and records retention. |
| Legal counsel | Reviews legal terms, deviations, addenda, high-risk positions, and escalation decisions. |
| Finance / procurement | Confirms budget, payment terms, PO alignment, tax, and DoA compliance. |
| Privacy / security | Reviews data processing, system access, security controls, subprocessors, and breach obligations. |
| Authorized signatory | Signs only after final version and approval trail are complete. |

## Evidence Required
- Completed intake record.
- Risk rating and review lane.
- Final approved redline or clean contract.
- Approval trail and DoA evidence.
- Executed copy and signature envelope.
- Contract register record.
- Obligation tracker entries.
- Renewal notice schedule.
- Retention/archive location.

## Assessment Checklist
- Is the business owner named and accountable?
- Is the counterparty legal entity correct?
- Is the contract value recorded and approved?
- Is the risk rating supported by the risk matrix?
- Are non-standard terms documented?
- Are privacy, security, finance, procurement, and legal approvals complete where needed?
- Is the signed version linked in the register?
- Are obligations and renewal dates tracked?

## Common Gaps
- Contract requests submitted without value, owner, or target date.
- Customer paper reviewed without a deviation summary.
- Signed contracts stored outside the register.
- Renewal dates entered but notice dates omitted.
- Obligations left in legal language without operational owners.
- PO amendments used for material changes that require addenda and DoA approval.

## Recommended Improvements
- Require mandatory intake fields before review starts.
- Use a standard clause playbook for repeat negotiation issues.
- Add monthly checks for incomplete register fields.
- Automate renewal alerts at 120, 90, 60, and 30 days.
- Review high-risk exceptions quarterly and update templates.
- Train business owners on when contract changes require formal approval.
